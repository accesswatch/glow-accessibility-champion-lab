# GLOW Framework

GLOW is the organizing framework for this capstone initiative. Each letter describes a phase in a human-centered, AI-supported accessibility workflow.

## G — Ground

Ground the work in a real accessibility problem.

Start with the partner's actual need, not the AI tool. What specific barrier, document type, or communication challenge is in front of them? The problem must be real, bounded, and appropriate for AI assistance.

## L — Learn

Learn what people need to understand.

Identify the accessibility pattern behind the barrier. What principle, standard, or practice applies? This is where the educational dimension of the workflow lives. The goal is not to fix the content for the partner. The goal is to help the partner understand the pattern so they can improve future work.

## O — Organize

Organize a repeatable workflow.

Turn the pattern into a structured, teachable workflow: a prompt, a checklist, a template, or a lab activity. The workflow should include role, task, trusted guidance, output format, and human review. It should work without requiring the partner to be an AI expert.

## W — Walk Forward

Walk forward as accessibility champions.

Leave the interaction with a practical next step, a reusable artifact, and clarity about what requires human review. The partner should be more confident, not more dependent on AI.

## GLOW and AI

AI tools can support the Learn and Organize phases by drafting examples, explaining patterns, and generating checklist options. Human expertise is required for the Ground and Walk Forward phases, and human review is always required before any AI-generated content is used with real partners.
