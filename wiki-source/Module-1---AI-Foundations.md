# Module 1 - AI Foundations

## What I Learned

Module 1 helped me distinguish between language generation and trustworthy
knowledge. Large language models can produce useful explanations, but they do
not own truth, context, policy, or accessibility responsibility.

## Capstone Connection

For the GLOW Accessibility Champion Lab, AI is not the center of the system.
Human-centered accessibility capacity building is the center. AI can support
drafts, examples, checklists, and coaching, but every meaningful accessibility
decision requires human review.

### Text Description

The following describes the same relationships shown in the diagram above.
It serves as the accessible long description (WCAG 1.1.1) for contexts where
the image cannot be perceived.

**Technical spine** — each step is a narrower subset or application of the
one before, with the relationship type in parentheses:

- Artificial Intelligence (the broad field)
  - *includes* Machine Learning (learns patterns from data)
    - *uses* Deep Learning (multi-layer networks)
      - *uses* Neural Networks (e.g., transformers)
        - *underlies* Large Language Models (e.g., Claude, GPT-4o)
          - *is trained as* Foundation Models (broad pre-training)
            - *enables* Generative AI (e.g., Claude.ai, ChatGPT)

**From generation to responsible use:**

- Generative AI *produces* Accessibility Drafts and Explanations.
- Drafts *require* Human Review (the required checkpoint).
- Human Review *checks*: Accuracy, Privacy, Bias and Fairness.
- Human Review *applies*: Accessibility Expertise.

**The GLOW path — the human-centered core:**

- GLOW Workflows *structure* Human Review.
- GLOW Workflows *train* Campus Partners.
- Campus Partners *become* Accessibility Champions.

**Key relationship:** The center of the map is human-centered accessibility
capacity building, not the AI model. GLOW workflows translate broad AI
capability into narrow, teachable, human-reviewed accessibility practice.
