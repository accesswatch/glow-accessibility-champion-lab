# Human Review and Accessibility Safeguards

## The Human Review Model

Human review is not optional in GLOW workflows. It is the central safeguard that makes AI assistance responsible.

Every GLOW workflow includes a defined human-review checkpoint. The checkpoint must be specific: who reviews, what they check, and what happens if something is wrong.

## What Human Review Covers

- **Accuracy**: Are WCAG, policy, and legal claims correct and attributed to trusted sources?
- **Accessibility quality**: Does the output actually improve accessibility, or does it just look like it does?
- **Context**: Is the guidance appropriate for this specific partner, document, and situation?
- **Privacy**: Does the output contain or imply any sensitive information?
- **Bias**: Does the output treat disabled people respectfully and avoid generalizations?
- **Tone**: Is the output supportive, practical, and non-shaming?

## What AI Must Not Decide

- Final accessibility compliance determinations
- Accommodation decisions or accommodation-specific guidance
- Legal interpretation of Section 508, ADA, Title II, or related law
- Policy decisions affecting specific students or employees
- Medical, diagnostic, or disability-related determinations

## Accountability

A named person or role must own the final output. AI-assisted content is not a product of the AI. It is the product of the person who reviewed and approved it.

## Disclosure

When AI assistance materially shaped public-facing guidance, consider including a brief disclosure. Example: "This guidance was developed with AI assistance and reviewed by a human accessibility professional."
