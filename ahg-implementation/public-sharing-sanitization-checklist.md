# Public Sharing Sanitization Checklist

Use this checklist before sharing any GLOW Accessibility Champion Lab content publicly, including wiki publication, AHG workshop handouts, conference presentations, and repository release.

## Content Review

- [ ] All examples use public, fictional, or sanitized content.
- [ ] No student, employee, or accommodation information is present.
- [ ] No medical, legal, or HR-related details are present.
- [ ] No internal organizational details are present that were not intended for public sharing.
- [ ] No unpublished research or protected intellectual property is present.

## Accessibility Claims

- [ ] WCAG claims are accurate and attributed to the W3C/WAI source.
- [ ] Legal claims are appropriately scoped and not presented as legal advice.
- [ ] Accommodation and disability-related claims are reviewed by a qualified person.
- [ ] Automated checking is not presented as sufficient for compliance.

## Disability and Equity

- [ ] Disability language is respectful, current, and not patronizing.
- [ ] Disabled people are not treated as a monolith.
- [ ] Multiple disability and access experiences are acknowledged where relevant.
- [ ] The content does not require users to identify as disabled to benefit.

## AI Transparency

- [ ] AI use is disclosed where it materially shaped the content.
- [ ] Human review is described clearly.
- [ ] Outputs are not presented as authoritative without human verification.
